Why It Matters

Vaping isn't the safe alternative it's marketed to be

21%

Canadian students in grades 7–12 uses a vaping product

Source: CSTADS
69%

Youth who vaped had never smoked a traditional cigarette

Source: Health Canada
31%

Youth 15–19 who vape, cite "vaping to reduce stress" as their reason

Source: Canada.ca

Vaping among Canadian youth has reached alarming levels, with many teens unaware of the risks and addictive nature of nicotine. The misconception that vaping is a "safe" alternative to smoking has contributed to its widespread adoption among teenagers who have never touched a cigarette.

What many don't realize is that Canadian e-liquids contain nicotine levels up to three times higher than European limits, significantly increasing addiction risk. Early nicotine exposure impairs developing brains, affecting memory, attention, and impulse control.
